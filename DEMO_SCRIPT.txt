================================================================================
MEMORY GUARDIAN - 3-MINUTE DEMO SCRIPT
================================================================================

[Visual: Show assets/cover_page_banner.png - Cybernetic shield protecting a brain]

--- 1. THE HOOK (0:00 - 0:30) ---
[Speaker Tone: Engaging, relatable, and direct]

"How many browser tabs do you have open right now? How late did you stay up scrolling last night? 

In our hyper-connected world, we monitor our servers, our databases, and our API latencies. But we ignore our most critical processor: our own brain. 

Our modern digital habits—infinite scroll, sleep deprivation, and constant screen fatigue—are quietly driving attention fragmentation and cognitive decline. 

Introducing Memory Guardian: a safety-first, multi-agent AI assistant designed to monitor digital lifestyle metrics and safeguard your long-term cognitive health."

--- 2. PURPOSE & VALUE (0:30 - 1:00) ---
"Memory Guardian doesn't just track data; it calculates a personalized Cognitive Risk Score and enforces rigid safety guardrails. Built on Google's ADK 2.0 framework and the Model Context Protocol (MCP), it is privacy-first, modular, and stateful."

--- 3. ARCHITECTURE & MCP (1:00 - 1:45) ---
[Visual: Show assets/architecture_diagram.png - Stateful multi-agent graph]

"Let’s look under the hood. Memory Guardian uses a stateful directed graph workflow:
First, a central ConciergeAgent orchestrates the execution. 
It delegates telemetry-gathering tasks to three parallel sub-agents: the WebActivityAnalyzer, the ScreenTimeAgent, and the HealthRecoveryAgent.
These sub-agents communicate with an isolated Model Context Protocol (MCP) server running FastMCP over stdio to fetch synthetic user telemetry. 
Once metrics are gathered, our RiskScoringAgent calculates an overall Cognitive Risk Score. 
By delegating tasks to dedicated sub-agents, we avoid prompt bloat and keep each component highly focused."

--- 4. THE SECURITY CHECKPOINT & HITL (1:45 - 2:30) ---
"But AI needs safety boundaries. Before any advice reaches the user, the workflow passes through a strict Security Checkpoint:
1. PII Redaction: Any cognitive journals are scanned to automatically scrub emails, phone numbers, or SSNs.
2. Prompt Injection Defense: It blocks command override attempts.
3. Risk-Based Gating: 
   - A score of 50 or less is Low Risk and auto-approves.
   - A score between 50 and 70 is Medium Risk, which auto-approves but interrupts to request a custom detailed summary if requested.
   - A score above 70 is High Risk. It halts execution and triggers a Human-in-the-Loop (HITL) gate using ADK’s stateful resumability. The system saves its state to disk, suspends, and waits for explicit user approval."

--- 5. LIVE DEMO WALKTHROUGH (2:30 - 3:15) ---
"Let's see the demo in action using the agents-cli playground.
In our first scenario, the user sleeps 6.5 hours and has 4 hours of screen time. The scoring agent computes a Medium Risk score of 65. The security checkpoint passes automatically, and the Memory Guard Agent successfully delivers customized wellness recommendations.
In our second scenario, the user has only 4 hours of sleep and high screen time. The Risk Score spikes to 90. 
Watch as the terminal halts. The checkpoint suspends the execution and prompts: 'Checkpoint approval required.'
Once the user submits a JSON payload approving the action, the ADK reloads the state from disk and safely resumes the workflow to deliver the finalized recommendations."

--- 6. CLOSING (3:15 - 3:30) ---
"Memory Guardian shows the power of ADK 2.0: combining stateful multi-agent workflows, isolated MCP tool integration, and deterministic safety checkpoints. It’s AI that looks out for your mind—without compromising your privacy. Thank you."
